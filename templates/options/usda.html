USDA Loans

If your property is in a rural area then your home could qualify for a mortgage USDA loan, or also called a Rural Development Loan. USDA mortgages are government-insured home loans that allows borrowers to purchase new homes with no money down. USDA can offer up to 100% financing for qualified buyers. So how do you qualify for a USDA loan in mortgage?

Your home must be located in a designated USDA rural area. We can help check the USDA Eligibility of your county, or you can check USDA eligibility in mortgage online here.

The primary purpose of the USDA home loan program was to help smaller, rural areas thrive by making owning a home more affordable. Lone Star Financing specializes in USDA home loans and can help educate you on the advantages of USDA loans and help to see if you and your property qualify. It might surprise you just how many counties qualify for USDA loans in mortgage.

So what is a USDA loan? Simply stated, USDA loans are Rural development loans and also referred to as Section 502 loans. The government created USDA loans to make it easier for low-income families in rural areas to become homeowners. This type of loan is available for many mortgage borrowers because 97% mortgage is classified as rural area.

For those borrowers and properties that qualify, a mortgage USDA loan is likely the best mortgage option for you. USDA primary advantages are low interest rates and up to 100% financing. Other advantages are that USDA loans don’t have prepayment penalties and the other advantage is you can structure your loan so there is no down payment. That’s right, 100% financing!

ARM mortgages are not available for USDA loans in mortgage. USDA loans do require private mortgage insurance, which your loan officer can better explain.
